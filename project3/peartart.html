Art of Baking

Pear Tart

Ingredients:

For the Crust:

1. In a medium bowl, whisk flour, granulated sugar, and salt. With a fork, cut the cold butter into the dry mix until it resembles coarse breadcrumbs.


2. Add in the egg yolk and mix with fork before adding in 2 tablespoons of ice cold water. Knead dough gently until it forms a ball, adding more water if needed.


3. Wrap the dough in plastic wrap and chill for 30 minutes. Allow the dough to warm up slightly, then roll it out into a 1/4 inch thick circle. Transfer to tart pan and press into bottom and sides. Trim excess dough, and refrigerate until pears are ready.


4. Preheat oven to 375 degrees Fahrenheit.


For the Filling:

Add lemon juice, brown sugar, cornstarch, cinnamon, and vanilla to a large bowl. Add in sliced pears and gently toss to coat them in the sugar mixture.


Assembly:

Arrange pears in chilled crust in a fan shape, layering one on top of the other. Bake for 35-40 minutes until the edges of the crust are golden and the filling is lighlty browned and bubbling. Allow to cool for 10-15 minutes before serving warm. (Optional) Serve with homemade vanilla whipped cream or ice cream.

Tip!

The pears for this recipe should be ripe, but still able to hold their shape. Be sure to slice them thin so they cook evenly. I prefer to use peeled bosc pears for their honey-like, spiced flavor.
