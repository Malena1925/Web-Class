Art of Baking

Carrot Cake

Tip!

Feel free to add other additions to your carrot cake! Some options could include canned crushed pineapple (just be sure to account for additional moisture), candied ginger, or pecans. For more flavor, add a tablespoon of orange juice and zest each to the frosting.

Ingredients:

For the Cake:

1.Preheat over to 400 degrees Fahrenheit. Grease and flour two 9" x 2" round cake pans. Line bottoms with parchment paper.


2. Beat granulated sugar, oil, eggs on medium high for 2 minutes. Stir in vanilla.


3. In a separate bowl, sift together 2 cups of flour, cinnamon, baking soda, and salt. On low speed, slowly add the flour mixture to the sugar mixture.


4. In a medium bowl, combine carrots, walnuts, and raisins. Coat with remaining 1 tablespoon of flour. Fold this into batter, then divide batter between the two prepared pans. Smooth the tops and bake for 10 minutes.


5. After the first 10 minutes, reduce the oven temperature to 350 degrees Fahrenheit and bake for 30 to 35 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. Allow cakes to cool in pan for 15 minutes, then transfer to wire rack to cool completely.


For the Frosting:

1. In a large bowl, beat cream cheese and butter until smooth. Add vanilla and salt.


2. Add powdered sugar 1 cup at a time, scraping the sides of the bowl with a rubber spatula as needed. Continue until you have the desired consistency. For thicker frosting, add more powdered sugar. To thin out frosting, add in a tablespoon or two of heavy whipping cream or milk.


Assembly:

Place first cake rounded side down onto a flat serving plate. spread a third of the frosting onto the top before adding the second cake (rounded side up) on top. Use the remaining frosting to cover the rest of the cake. (Optional) Decorate with candied walnuts, candied ginger, or dye any remaining frosting to pipe miniature carrots on top.
