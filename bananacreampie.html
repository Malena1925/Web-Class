Banana Cream Pie

Tip!

You can use a store-bought or homemade pre-baked crust. You could even use a graham craker or nilla wafer crust for a completely no-bake pie!

I prefer a traditional crust. My go-to recipe is 1 1/2 cups of all-purpose flour, 1/2 tablespoon of granulated sugar, 1/2 teaspoon of salt, and 1 stick of cold, unsalted butter. "Pinch" the ingredients together using your fingers until the largest butter pieces are no bigger than a walnut. Slowly add in ice cold water tablespoon at a time until the mixture just sticks together when squeezed. Chill the dough before rolling it out. Bake lined with parchment paper and filled with baking beans to keep its shape.

Ingredients:

For the Filling:

1.In a heat proof bowl, whisk the eggs.


2. In a medium saucepan, combine the granulated sugar, cornstarch, and salt. Add in milk and stir until there are no clumps. Place over medium-high heat, whisking constantly until the mixture begins to bubble and thicken--around 6-8 minutes. Remove from heat.


3. Temper the eggs by whisking in one cup of the milk mixture, drizzling it into the egg bowl while constantly stirring. Be careful not to scramble the eggs. Slowly pour the egg mixture into the pot while whisking. Return to medium heat.


4. Gently whisk while cooking until it begins to bubble again. Cook the mixture until the whisk leaves a trail, about 6-8 minutes. Remove from heat.


5. Add the butter and vanilla and whisk to combine. The pudding may loosen, but this is normal! Transfer to another bowl and cover with plastic wrap. Allow the pudding to cool for 30 minutes before refrigerating overnight.


For the Cream:

Combine heavy whipping cream, 2 teaspoons of vanilla, and powdered sugar. Using a handmixer, beat on medium until stiff peaks form--about 3 minutes.


Assembly:

Add one cup of pudding into crust. Layer sliced bananas, and cover with more pudding. Repeat. Scoop whipped cream over top. (Optional) Cover with crushed graham crakers or nilla wafers!
